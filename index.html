Welcome to OpenSkiAI

Why This Idea?

The Inspiration

While skiing in Vals, Italy, I encountered a public ski racecourse that allowed anyone with a lift pass to ski around gates and record their performance. Watching my runs online inspired me to think: What if AI could analyze this data to give real-time coaching? This idea became OpenSkiAI—a project to make skiing accessible to everyone, regardless of income or background.

Why Open Source?

As a solopreneur, I’ve been licensing my music under Creative Commons licenses for many years. I understand the importance of making ideas and tools freely available. By creating OpenSkiAI under an open-source license, I hope to ensure that these basic, common-sense ideas remain free for anyone to use and expand upon—rather than being monopolized by corporations.

Preventing Innovation Barriers

In the past, corporations have patented simple concepts—like eBay’s “Make an Offer” button—which stifled innovation and prevented others from building upon them. By keeping OpenSkiAI free, we can democratize skiing and ensure that anyone, anywhere, can enjoy real-time AI coaching without facing financial or technological barriers.

Who Would Use This App

This app is designed to be as user-friendly and open-source as possible, empowering a wide range of users to configure the application in ways that suit their needs. Whether you’re a ski instructor, an individual skier, or part of a ski resort, this app would adapt to your unique requirements, providing insights and AI-driven analysis.

1. Ski Instructors

  • Create customized agents for their classes, allowing students to upload videos of their runs.
  • Receive AI-generated analysis of each student’s performance and provide tailored feedback.
  • Collaborate with students to track progress and refine techniques.

2. Individual Skiers

  • Interact directly with their personal AI agent to analyze their runs.
  • Compare performance with professional skiers and receive actionable advice.
  • Configure the app independently for personal improvement.

3. Ski Resorts

  • Enhance their offerings by integrating the app into public ski racecourses.
  • Provide feedback to users about their runs.
  • Ensure fair compensation to ski instructors for contributing data to train AI models.
